Perfect Grilled Chicken

Published December 1, 2017

This method takes 20 minutes, start to finish. I do this several times a week to always have chicken available. In this recipe, I split the chicken breasts in order to have thinner pieces to grill, keeping the cooking time down and cooking all the way through. To get a better sear, make sure the grill is on high when you add the chicken. Also, cooking the first side a little longer results in a better sear.

Ingredients

  • Boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • Olive oil
  • Course salt
  • Fresh ground pepper
  • Fresh thyme branches

Steps

  1. Turn your grill on high. Set a timer for 10 minutes (or the time it takes to get to maximum temp).
  2. Butterfly the chicken breast. Lie the chicken flat and cut across the breast, dividing the piece into two, equal width pieces.
  3. Pat the chicken breast dry with paper towels.
  4. Place the chicken in a wide but shallow bowl (a pie plate is perfect). Salt and pepper the chicken. Add olive oil to thoroughly coat.
  5. With the grill on high, place chicken on the grill. Cook for 4 minutes then flip and cook for 2 minutes. (Add time on the flip side for thicker cuts.)
  6. Remove chicken from grill and place on a plate layered with fresh thyme sprigs.
  7. Rest a few minutes before serving.
  8. If you are not serving right away, refridgerate the chicken without a cover. This retains the texture of the seared outside. Plastic wrap the plate later.

Tips

  • I buy chicken in bulk and freeze it. Do not leave meat on the counter to defront. This can lead to bacteria growth. Instead, let defrost in the refridgerator for a few days.
  • Let chicken rest on counter for a bit before cooking. This brings it to room temperature, preventing uneven, inside/outside cooking. Some recommend 30 minutes. I do about 15.
  • When butterflying chicken, try to make each half as even as possible. Uneven sizes make it harder to cook each piece the same temperature.
  • I use the alarm on my phone for precise timing.
  • Try swapping out the fresh thyme with fresh tarragon, rosemary or basil for a different flavor profile. You can also try mixing different herbs for a more varied flavor.
  • When slicing chicken for a salad or sandwich, I use a big chef's knife to make definitive cuts. Avoid jigsawing across the chicken several times. This makes a mess of the meat.
  • When dealing with raw chicken, wash your hands frequently.
