Roasted Vegetables

Published December 1, 2017

This technique can be used to roast so many different types of vegetables – asparagus, brussel sprouts, cauliflower, broccoli, carrots, squashes, onions, peppers, potatoes, sweet potatoes and more. The basic idea is coat the vegetables in olive oil, course salt and fresh pepper and then cook them on a cookie sheet lined with parchment (not wax) paper.

Ingredients

  • Root and/or sturdy vegetables
  • Olive oil
  • Course salt
  • Fresh ground pepper
  • Optional: fresh herbs

Steps

  1. Pre-heat your oven to 450.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, coat the cut vegetables in olive oil, course salt and fresh ground pepper.
  3. Line a cookie sheet with parchment (not wax) paper.
  4. Arrange vegetables in the cookie sheet so they are not overlapping. Overlapping vegetables do not cook, they steam. (Wet vegetables are not nice.)
  5. Cook for about 20 minutes or until vegetables start to brown and/or carmelize. Remove from oven and let cool. Handling right away might result in broken (or mushy) vegetables. Give them time to rest.

Tips

  • Make sure to cut your vegetables so all are the same basic size. This will cook more evenly.
  • Optional, but nice for texture: when vegetables are done cooking, remove them from the cookie sheet and let cool on a cooling (cookie) rack. The texture firms up. At this point you can use in another recipe or on top of a salad.
